OCTAL Operational Framework

Executive Overview

OCTAL (JTTF 9983) serves as a strategic integration force for U.S. national security across Central & South America. Blending IC, SOCOM, and interagency assets, it combats human trafficking, terrorism, and drug trafficking with multi-domain operations. HQ is based in Daytona Beach, FL with global staging and legal protection under a State Department IT contract.

Mission Objectives

  • Threat Neutralization: Eliminate trafficking-terrorism convergence.
  • Logistical Disruption: Shift narcotics flow to air-based corridors.
  • Strategic Influence: Deploy soft power and covert action to weaken adversaries.

Capabilities

  • Federated C2 under DARPA-encrypted network.
  • 2,000 JTTF-linked personnel + elite JSOC operators.
  • High-tech SOCs and EMP-resistant FOBs disguised as grave sites.
  • Psychological and cyber warfare operations powered by DARPA and State Dept IT contract.

Operational Identity

Symbolized by the cuttlefish—stealth, precision, and ambush—and flanked by the lion and Irish wolfhound. OCTAL uses layered cover identities, civil infiltration, and consular immunity to execute black ops undetected across hostile environments.
